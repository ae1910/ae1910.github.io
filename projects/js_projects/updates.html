Final Site: The Top Week 4

With what I learned about javascript, I felt that I could now make a site that incorporates what I already knew and what I recently learned.

For the site, I decided I wanted to create a fashion magazine much like Vogue, which will be called The Top. The main focus when creating the site will be its design and the use of an API. The API that will be used is the New York Times' Top Stories API. This returns an array of articles currently on the specified section, which in this case is the fashion section.

Creating the Proof of Concept

The first thing I did was create a wireframe of the site to get a basic idea of how the pages would look like. I used Adobe XD to create the wireframe.

After several tweaks, this was the result of some of the pages:

The main pages will be the homepage, the pages for the articles, and the search results page. I also plan to create an "About" page and a "Contact Us" page. As well, I plan to make the design mobile friendly.

When you first open the site, you are open to a slider that shows the thumbnail of the 4 most recent articles from the API. As you scroll, you will see a grid of the next 12 recent articles. At the end of the page will be a footer, that will include the option to "sign up' for the newsletter of the magazine.

When an article is chosen, you are taken to the page of that article. The first part of the page will be divided in half. One half with an image connected to the article and the other half will be the actual content of the article, to create a balance between the image and the text. The half of the page with the text would be scrollable, and, when you get to the end, you will see a section with options to see another article with the footer after. This section will be unveiled as if it were coming from under the part of the page that is half the article and the image. This idea came after watching a YouTube video from a YouTuber called Hyperplexed.

A feature I plan to also add to the site is a search function that allows you to search for an article. The results will show up on the search results page.
